          📖 Core Terminology & Physics

          Porosity & Permeability

          Porosity: The void space between grains holding fluid.
          Permeability (k): The ability of fluids to flow through interconnected pores. Visible as the blue paths moving through the matrix above.

          Hydrostatic Pressure (HP)

          The pressure exerted purely by the weight of a stationary column of fluid. Notice how the pressure vectors (arrows) increase in magnitude with depth (TVD).

          HP = 0.052 × MW × TVD

          Formation Pressure (FP)

          The pressure exerted by fluids contained in the pore spaces of rock. If FP (Red) exceeds Wellbore Pressure (Blue), a kick occurs.

          Pressure Gradients

          The rate at which pressure increases with vertical depth (psi/ft). Normal saltwater is ~0.465 psi/ft.

          Abnormal (Overpressure):

          Requires cap rock. Causes: Under-compaction of shales (most common), Artesian effect, Gas caps.

          Subnormal:

          Primarily caused by reservoir depletion. High risk of severe lost circulation.

          Bottom Hole Pressure (U-Tube)

          Static BHP:

          Pumps off. Fluid levels balance. BHP = HP.

          Dynamic BHP (Pumps On):

          The pump applies pressure (red arrow) down the drill pipe. This extra pressure overcomes friction and pushes fluid up the annulus, dynamically increasing bottom hole pressure (ECD).

          Circulating Friction & ECD

          Friction pressure is consumed progressively as mud travels the circuit:

          1. Surface Lines (1-5%): Standpipe, kelly hose, top drive.

          2. Drill String (20-30%): Inside drill pipe & BHA.

          3. Bit Nozzles (50-60%): Largest Loss Creates hydraulic impact. Does not affect BHP.

          4. Annulus / APL (5-10%): Most Important Flowing up against the rock. This acts downwards, directly adding to static HP to create Equivalent Circulating Density (ECD).

          Surface Pressure Limits

          MAASPMaximum Allowable Annular Surface Pressure

          The absolute maximum static pressure that can be applied to the annulus at the surface before the weakest point in the wellbore (usually the casing shoe) fractures. Based on Leak-Off Test (LOT) data.

          MASPMaximum Anticipated Surface Pressure

          The highest pressure predicted to occur at the surface during a worst-case well control event (e.g., evacuating gas to surface). Used primarily for equipment design and specification.

          MAWPMaximum Allowable Working Pressure

          The manufacturer's maximum physical pressure rating for surface equipment (BOP, manifold, valves) under normal operating conditions.

          📘 Tripping Mechanics & Pumping Out

          Statistically, the majority of well control incidents occur during tripping operations.

          Rigorous Volumetric Monitoring

          • Monitor displacement every 5 stands (DP), 3 stands (HWDP).
          • Stripping Tanks & Agitation: Use a dedicated stripping tank.
          • Prohibited: Never use a centrifugal pump to continuously fill from the suction pit.

          String Configuration

          • Bit Balling: Acts like a piston and multiplies swabbing suction forces.
          • Pumping Out: For complex wells, perform POOH while simultaneously pumping mud to break gel strength.

          🕵️‍♂️ Masking Factors (False Positives/Negatives)

          • 🔀
            Mud Transfers & AdditionsTransferring mud between active and reserve pits, or aggressively adding chemicals, alters surface volumes and completely obscures real pit gains or losses.
          • 🌊
            Flowline Drain BackWhen pumps are shut off, mud in the elevated flowline naturally drains back into the active pit. This expected, finite return can trigger a false pit gain alarm.
          • 🌡️
            Thermal ExpansionCold mud pumped downhole heats up upon circulation and expands in the annulus, causing a gradual pit gain that simulates a slow influx.
          • ⚖️
            U-Tubing (Heavy Slugs)Pumping a heavy pill causes the fluid level inside the drill pipe to drop rapidly while pushing fluid up the annulus. If not isolated to the trip tank, this return flow mimics a kick.

          🎈 Ballooning vs. True Kick

          Rule: Ballooning flow is FINITE and decelerates. Kick flow is CONTINUOUS and may accelerate.

          Wellbore Ballooning

          Micro-fractures absorb mud (due to ECD), then bleed it back slowly when pumps stop.

          True Kick

          Formation pressure exceeds Hydrostatic. Results in uninterrupted, continuous flow.

          Bleed-Off Test Verification

          Shut the well in and observe SIDPP. Bleed off a small, specific volume (e.g., 5 bbls). If pressure builds back to the exact same original SIDPP, it's a true kick. If pressure bleeds down and stays down, it's ballooning.

          ⏱️ Flow Check Guide

          A flow check eliminates dynamic variables (Equivalent Circulating Density and string movement) to reveal if static hydrostatic pressure alone can balance formation pressure.

          🔧

          1. Preparation & Alignment

          • 1. Isolate the Active Pit: Stop all mud transfers, chemical additions, and dumping.
          • 2. Align to Trip Tank: For accurate measurement, line up the return flow to the trip tank instead of the massive active pit system.
          • 3. Account for Drain Back: Wait a few minutes for mud in the elevated flowline to naturally drain back before taking a baseline reading.
          🕳️

          2. Execution (Drilling)

          • 1. Stop Rotary & Space Out: Halt rotation. Pick up off bottom to ensure a tool joint is not across the BOP rams.
          • 2. Stop Mud Pumps: Shut down pumps gradually. This removes ECD, relying solely on static Hydrostatic Pressure.
          • 3. Observe: Monitor the trip tank or bell nipple strictly for 10 to 15 minutes. Do not rush this step.
          🏗️

          2. Execution (Tripping)

          • 1. Set Slips: Immediately secure the drill string in the rotary table.
          • 2. Prepare FOSV: Ensure the Full Opening Safety Valve (TIW) is available and in the OPEN position.
          • 3. Observe: Watch the annulus or trip tank. If the well begins to flow actively, immediately stab the FOSV, close it, and initiate Hard Shut-In.

          3. Interpretation & Decision Matrix

          🚨 Result: POSITIVE (Flowing)

          Continuous flow is observed out of the wellbore after the flowline has completely drained back.

          Action: Immediate Shut-In
          ✅ Result: NEGATIVE (Static)

          Fluid level remains completely static after initial drain back. Hydrostatic pressure is sufficient.

          Action: Resume Operations (Monitor)

          ⚠️ RP-59 Kick Diagnostics

          Differentiate between Warning Signs and Positive Indicators based on operational context.

          Drilling

          ⚠️ Warning Signs

          May indicate influx. Verification required.

          • ■ Drilling Break (sudden ROP increase)
          • ■ Increased Torque & Drag
          • ■ Connection Gas / Background Gas Spikes
          • ■ Decrease in pump pressure / Increase in SPM
          • ■ Decrease in D-Exponent
          Action: Perform Flow Check
          Drilling

          🚨 Positive Indicators

          Confirmed influx. Immediate action required.

          • ■ Flow Rate Increase (Pumps constant)
          • ■ Pit Volume Gain
          • ■ Well flowing with pumps OFF
          Action: Immediate Hard Shut-In
          Tripping

          ⚠️ Warning Signs

          May indicate swabbing or surging effects.

          • ■ Excessive Overpull / Drag
          • ■ Trip tank level deviations (minor)
          • ■ Gas cut mud on bottom up circulation
          Action: Perform Flow Check
          Tripping

          🚨 Positive Indicators

          Confirmed influx during pipe movement.

          • ■ Hole fails to take calculated mud volume (POOH)
          • ■ Hole returns more mud than calculated (RIH)
          • ■ Well actively flowing from annulus or inside pipe
          Action: Install FOSV & Shut-In

          🛑 API Standard Shut-In Procedures

          Rigidly aligned with API RP-59, emphasizing the Hard Shut-In methodology to absolutely minimize influx volume during a well control event.

          🕳️ While Drilling

          1. 1
            Sound Alarm.
          2. 2
            Stop rotating & pick up off bottom.Space out carefully to ensure a tool joint is not positioned across the BOP rams.
          3. 3
            Stop Mud Pumps.
          4. 4
            Close Annular Preventer or Upper Pipe Rams.Confirm choke is fully closed prior to this step (Core requirement of Hard Shut-in).
          5. 5
            Open HCR (Hydraulic Control line) Valve.
          6. 6
            Notify Supervisor & Monitor.Record SIDPP (Drill Pipe), SICP (Casing), and exactly how much pit gain occurred.

          🏗️ While Tripping

          1. 1
            Sound Alarm & Set Slips immediately.
          2. 2
            Stab Full Opening Safety Valve (FOSV/TIW).Crucial: Must be in the OPEN position to allow fluid passage during stabbing against pressure.
          3. 3
            Close the FOSV.
          4. 4
            Close Annular Preventer or Pipe Rams.
          5. 5
            Open HCR Valve.Then make up Top Drive or Inside BOP to the string.
          6. 6
            Open FOSV & Monitor.Notify Supervisor. Record stabilized pressures (SIDPP/SICP).

          ⏱️ API Well Control Drills

          Drills train crews in rapid barrier reinstatement to build muscle memory. API requires reaction times to be rigorously practiced and recorded.

          🕳️

          Pit Drill (Drilling)

          Frequency: Routine / Before HP Zones
          1. Supervisor discreetly raises float to simulate pit gain (alarm sounds).
          2. Driller recognizes alarm, immediately stops rotation, and picks up to space out tool joints.
          3. Stop mud pumps.
          4. Shut the well in (simulate closing BOP).
          Target API Reaction Time ≤ 1 Min
          🏗️

          Trip Drill (Tripping)

          Frequency: Routine
          1. Supervisor simulates incorrect fill volume on the trip tank.
          2. Driller halts operation and sets slips immediately.
          3. Crew safely stabs FOSV (must be in open position), then strictly closes it.
          4. Shut the well in (simulate closing BOP annular).
          Target API Reaction Time ≤ 2 Mins
          🛡️

          BOP Equipment Drill

          Frequency: Varies (Weekly/Monthly)

          Ensures crew familiarity with all BOP control panels (driller's panel, remote panel, accumulator unit). Practice aligning valves, identifying accumulator pressures, and function-testing components (where permitted without sealing on pipe).

          🎛️

          Choke Manipulation

          Frequency: Before Drilling Shoe

          Conducted strictly before drilling out casing shoe tracks. Involves discharging mud through the remote choke into the trip tank to calibrate crew response to choke adjustments and gauge lag time.
